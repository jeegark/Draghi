    Method and Sources

    Last updated: --

    • The counter starts at midnight on 9 September 2024 (UTC), the date the Draghi Report was published to the European Commission.
    • The annual cost trajectory follows a convex ramp calibrated to the Draghi Report's headline €800bn annual investment gap, reaching full run-rate by 2035. A power-law profile (exponent 0.7) reflects slow early accumulation that accelerates as structural divergences compound.
    • Implementation progress reduces costs by up to 75% per theme — a ceiling reflecting path dependency: once firms relocate, talent leaves, or competitors capture markets, some damage is irreversible even with full implementation.
    • Sub-theme baselines sum to €800bn/year. Each is weighted by its share of the investment gap as identified in Parts A and B of the Draghi Report.
    • After 2035, the model continues at the 2035 implied annual run rate until superseded by new data.
    • Reform statuses are evidence-led, cross-referenced with the Draghi Observatory (draghiobservatory.eu) and named official EU releases.
